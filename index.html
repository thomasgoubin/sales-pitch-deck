MISC · Appendix

Competitive Landscape

Feedback & Insights (single-source)
Productboard: Manual input, PMs must work inside their UI. Just launched Spark (Claude co-work) but synthesis happens in isolated chat threads, nothing carries over between sessions. Single-source.
Dovetail: Research repository, quali only, manual tagging. Great for UX research, not for cross-source product intelligence.
Enterpret: Auto-tags support tickets & reviews with ML. Good single-source NLP but doesn't connect to analytics, Slack, or sales calls. No cross-source synthesis.
Unwrap: Similar to Enterpret. Auto-categorizes feedback from one source at a time. No proactive reporting, no cross-source patterns.
Analytics (quanti only)
Amplitude / Mixpanel / PostHog: Quantitative only. No qualitative synthesis. PMs export charts and manually connect dots in docs.
Pendo: Usage analytics + in-app feedback. Closest to cross-source but still siloed: doesn't ingest Slack, Gong, Intercom, or Jira context.
DIY with AI
Claude / ChatGPT direct: No memory across sessions, no scheduling, no team workspace, no traceability. Works for one PM, breaks for a team.
Graphlit: Infrastructure layer (connectors, extraction, structured memory). Powerful but unopinionated: you still need to build the product layer, reports, scheduling, team features on top. 6+ months of eng work.
Engineering Intelligence (validates category)
Jellyfish ($114M raised): Does for engineering what Naptha does for product: makes invisible allocation patterns visible. Proves the category exists and enterprises pay for operational intelligence.
